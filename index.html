    3 December 2024 Fabien gives a seminar talk at the Center for Nonlinear Studies of the Los Alamos National Laboratory, New Mexico.
    23 November 2024 We are at the APS-DFD meeting in Salt Lake City this week, where Ashish presents our work on high-order semi-Lagrangian advection.

    11 October 2024 New paper published in the Journal of Computational Physics, in which we propose a correction scheme for recovering an accurate undisturbed velocity in two-way coupled wall-bounded Euler-Lagrange simulations (collab with Akshay Chandran and Berend van Wachem at the University of Magdeburg). The manuscript is also available on arXiv.
    04 October 2024 New paper published in the Journal of Fluid Mechanics with the excellent folks at the University of Magdeburg. We discuss several theoretical aspects of the volume-filtered Euler-Lagrange framework for simulating particle-laden flows. The manuscript can also be found on arXiv.
    17 September 2024 Lorenzo is presenting his work on PSI and crater modeling at the Lunar Surface Science Workshop, Virtual Session 25: Plume-Surface Interactions.
    15 August 2024 Welcome to Conrad Delgado, Ilia Kheirkhah, and Parin Trivedi, joining the group as PhD students! Conrad will be co-advised by Marco Panesi.
    25 June 2024 Fabien is presenting our work on the numerical transport of fluid moments at the ICNMMF conference in Reykjavík, Iceland.
    08 June 2024 Two new papers published in the Special Issue on Machine Learning for Multiphase Flows of the International Journal of Multiphase Flow. Paper 1 with Andrew Cahaly and Olivier Desjardins (Cornell) presents \(\texttt{PLIC-Net}\), a neural-network model for reconstructing piecewise-linear interfaces in volume-of-fluid simulations. Paper 2 with Hani Elmestikawy, Julia Reuter, Sanaz Mostaghim, and Berend van Wachem (University of Madgeburg) proposes a new deterministic drag model for dense particle laden flows in the Stokes regime, using a approach combining neural-networks and genetic programming.
